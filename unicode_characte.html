What are the characteristics of Unicode symbols?

Global: Unicode symbols are cross-lingual and cross-cultural, covering almost all languages and symbol systems in the world.

Uniformity: Unicode notation assigns a unique number to each character, avoiding conflicts and confusion between different encodings.

Extensibility: Unicode notation is constantly expanded and upgraded to support a variety of languages and emerging symbols.

Ease of use: Unicode notation can be entered and processed through programming interfaces or input methods, eliminating the need to consider the compatibility of multiple encodings.

Cross-platform: Unicode symbols can be used universally in different operating systems and software environments, ensuring consistency in text delivery and display.

Advantages of Unicode Symbols

The use of symbols can make code clean and beautiful and easier to read. Many programmers like to use symbols to comment code or apply them in the resulting output. In addition, symbols have become one of the most common and popular expressions in modern social media, chat and email. With Unicode, it is easy to add symbols to our applications, documents, and even everyday interactions. In many cases, special symbols can better convey the feeling, intent, and importance of a message.
